We'd rather you knew.

It's not your imagination — the average age of perimenopause onset has been declining. Women in their late 30s and early 40s are increasingly experiencing symptoms their mothers didn't see until their early 50s. Researchers point to the rise of endocrine-disrupting chemicals in everyday life as a significant contributing factor. PFAS compounds — found in non-stick cookware, food packaging, and drinking water — have been linked in published studies to perimenopause onset up to 2–4 years earlier in women with higher blood PFAS levels. Microplastics, now detectable in human blood, breast tissue, and ovarian follicles, contain BPA and phthalates that mimic estrogen and interfere with the hormonal signalling that regulates your cycle. Your body isn't failing you. It's responding to an environment it wasn't designed for. Magnolia was formulated with this in mind — every herb chosen to support hormonal resilience under modern conditions.
Endocrine disruptors are chemicals that interfere with your body's hormonal system by mimicking, blocking, or altering hormone signals. The most common ones are PFAS (found in non-stick pans, waterproof clothing, and food packaging), BPA and phthalates (found in many plastics), and pesticide residues. These chemicals can accelerate the depletion of ovarian follicles, disrupt the hypothalamic-pituitary axis that governs your cycle, and amplify the severity of menopause symptoms. Reducing exposure — switching to cast iron or stainless steel cookware, filtering drinking water, eating organic where possible — is meaningful. Pairing that with adaptogenic botanicals that support your liver's hormone detoxification pathways (schisandra, ashwagandha) and your adrenal function (shatavari, rhodiola) gives your body the best chance of navigating the transition smoothly.
All three Magnolia blends are hormone-free and designed to complement — not conflict with — HRT. That said, we always recommend discussing any new supplement regimen with your healthcare provider, especially if you are taking prescription medications.
